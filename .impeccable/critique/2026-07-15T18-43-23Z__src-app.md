## Overall Impression

This is a credible internal reporting instrument with unusually honest evidence handling. The largest opportunity is to preserve exhaustive reporting while making the on-screen experience more decision-oriented. The release-critical corrections are narrower: semantic yellow, responsive Measure rows, auth heading structure, focus/placeholder contrast, and selected-row treatment.

## What's Working

1. The information architecture is disciplined: exactly Overview, Reports, Data Entry, and Setup, with shallow navigation and clear orientation.
2. Evidence honesty is strong: missing values are not shown as zero; denominators, exclusions, targets, sources, calculation explanations, and qualified states remain visible.
3. The responsive task model is sound overall: navigation becomes a focus-managed drawer and Data Entry becomes a focused list/detail flow without losing URL context.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Full Board Report is expensive and difficult to traverse on screen

The required 59-Measure report renders roughly 6,600 elements and over 650 KB of decoded document content in the measured sample. Keep print/export complete, but reduce browser rendering cost immediately. A future product decision may add attention-first jump navigation or collapsible Priority/Goal sections without hiding evidence.

### [P1] Shared focus and placeholder tokens miss accessibility contrast

The translucent teal focus outline composites below 3:1 and the `#7a9aa3` placeholder is roughly 3.01:1 on white. Use the opaque primary teal focus token and the ink-500 placeholder token; keep meaning independent of color.

### [P1] Narrow Priority Measure rows collapse

At 320px the Measure name, value, Board status, and arrow are forced into one horizontal row, collapsing the name into a few characters per line. Stack the evidence on narrow screens and retain the compact horizontal row from `sm` upward.

### [P1] Auth pages lose their visible h1 on narrow screens

The identity-panel h1 is hidden below `lg`, leaving a visible h2 as the page's first heading on login and initial password setup. Make the form task the page h1 and treat the desktop identity statement as supporting content.

### [P2] Login spends the Sample-only yellow on decoration

The yellow highlight behind “clarity” contradicts the provenance contract and makes an internal access page feel like a marketing island. Remove it and keep the navy identity surface restrained.

### [P2] Data Entry's selected state uses a thick side-tab stripe

Replace `border-l-4` with a full selected surface and text/state emphasis through shared tokens.

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex, power user:** Data Entry offers limited queue acceleration across 53 Measures, the Board Report lacks section jumps, and equal-weight export actions require repeated parsing.

**Sam, keyboard/screen-reader/low-vision user:** Semantics, labels, live regions, 44px controls, and modal focus are strong. The current focus token and placeholder contrast fail, auth heading hierarchy breaks on narrow screens, and the fully expanded report creates a long traversal.

**Maya, executive decision reviewer:** “74 need attention” does not identify which conditions should change a meeting decision. Completeness is credible, but consequence and prioritization remain a product limitation rather than a safe final-pass inference.
